# IFRS 15 Five-Step Model

Use this model for full-IFRS entities and complex contracts requiring IFRS 15 analysis.

## Step 1: Identify the contract

Required:

- Approved parties and enforceable rights.
- Payment terms.
- Commercial substance.
- Probable collection.

## Step 2: Identify performance obligations

A promise is a separate performance obligation when the good or service is distinct:

- Customer can benefit from it on its own or with readily available resources.
- Promise is separately identifiable from other promises in the contract.

## Step 3: Determine transaction price

Include fixed consideration and estimate variable consideration using expected value or most-likely amount. Constrain variable consideration to amounts that are highly probable not to reverse materially.

## Step 4: Allocate transaction price

Allocate to performance obligations using relative stand-alone selling prices. Use observable prices where available; otherwise use adjusted-market, expected-cost-plus-margin, or residual approach when permitted.

## Step 5: Recognise revenue

| Pattern | Trigger |
|---|---|
| Over time | Customer receives and consumes benefits, customer controls asset as created, or asset has no alternative use and enforceable right to payment exists. |
| Point in time | Control transfers at delivery, acceptance, legal title, physical possession, or customer use, based on facts. |

## Contract balances

- Contract asset: performed but not yet unconditional right to consideration.
- Receivable: unconditional right to consideration.
- Contract liability: payment received or due before performance.

## Controls

- Principal-versus-agent assessment is required where third-party fulfilment exists.
- Service-type warranties are separate performance obligations.
- Significant financing components require separate accounting unless practical expedient applies.
