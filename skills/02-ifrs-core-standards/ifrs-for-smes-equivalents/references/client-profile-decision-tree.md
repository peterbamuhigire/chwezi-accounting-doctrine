# Client Profile Decision Tree

Use this decision tree to select IFRS for SMEs versus full IFRS for planning and implementation.

## Framework selection

| Step | Question | If yes | If no |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Is the entity publicly accountable, listed, a bank, insurer, pension, or regulated capital-market entity? | Full IFRS. | Go to step 2. |
| 2 | Does law, regulator, donor, lender, or group policy require full IFRS? | Full IFRS. | Go to step 3. |
| 3 | Is the entity preparing for listing, sale to a full-IFRS group, or major external financing? | Consider full IFRS with reviewer sign-off. | Go to step 4. |
| 4 | Is the entity a typical private SME, school, clinic, NGO, retail, hospitality, family business, or agribusiness? | IFRS for SMEs default. | Reviewer decision. |

## Implementation routing

| Profile signal | Default treatment |
|---|---|
| Simple leases and rentals | Section 20 unless full IFRS selected. |
| Simple receivables, payables, and loans | Section 11. |
| Complex financing, derivatives, or hedge accounting | Full IFRS review required. |
| Multi-element contracts or significant variable consideration | Check whether Section 23 is sufficient; full IFRS 15 may be needed by policy. |
| Donor-funded NGO | IFRS for SMEs unless donor agreement says otherwise. |
| Agriculture SME | Section 34 agriculture with fair-value evidence. |

## Required output

- Chosen framework.
- Reason for selection.
- Required reviewer role.
- Known framework-specific caveats.
- Modules affected by framework choice.
