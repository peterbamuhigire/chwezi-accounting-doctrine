# Section 11 Impairment for Basic Financial Instruments

IFRS for SMEs Section 11 uses an indicator-based impairment approach for basic financial instruments.

## Scope

- Trade receivables.
- Loan receivables.
- Deposits and simple debt instruments.
- Basic instruments measured at cost or amortised cost.

## Indicators

| Indicator | Evidence |
|---|---|
| Significant financial difficulty of debtor | Collection notes, insolvency notice, restructuring request. |
| Breach of contract | Default, delinquency, or missed payment. |
| Concession granted for economic or legal reasons | Restructured terms or payment holiday. |
| Probable bankruptcy or financial reorganisation | Legal filing or public notice. |
| Observable decrease in estimated future cash flows | Ageing trend, sector stress, or portfolio collection history. |

## Measurement

| Instrument | Impairment amount |
|---|---|
| Trade receivable | Carrying amount less recoverable amount. |
| Amortised-cost loan | Carrying amount less present value of estimated cash flows discounted at original effective interest rate. |
| Cost-measured equity instrument | Carrying amount less best estimate of recoverable amount. |

## Journal

| Account | Debit | Credit |
|---|---:|---:|
| Bad debts or impairment expense | Impairment loss | 0 |
| Allowance for doubtful debts or instrument allowance | 0 | Impairment loss |

## Controls

- Run impairment assessment at every reporting date.
- Do not use IFRS 9 Stage 1, Stage 2, or Stage 3 labels in an IFRS for SMEs book.
- Write-off requires evidence that collection is no longer expected and approval under credit policy.
