# Deferred-Tax Recognition Checklist

Use this checklist before recognising a deferred-tax asset or liability.

## Deferred-tax liability

| Question | Required conclusion |
|---|---|
| Does a taxable temporary difference exist? | Yes. |
| Is an exception applicable? | No, or documented with reviewer sign-off. |
| Is the tax rate source current and applicable? | Source-register state supports final use. |
| Is presentation P&L, OCI, or equity identified? | Yes. |

## Deferred-tax asset

| Question | Required conclusion |
|---|---|
| Does a deductible temporary difference, tax loss, or credit exist? | Yes. |
| Is future taxable profit probable? | Yes, supported by forecasts or reversal schedule. |
| Is the reversal period identified? | Yes, or caveated. |
| Is the tax rate source current and applicable? | Source-register state supports final use. |
| Is any unrecognised portion disclosed? | Yes, where material. |

## Evidence for probability

- Approved budgets or taxable-profit forecast.
- Reversal schedule for taxable temporary differences.
- Expiry dates for tax losses or credits.
- Tax planning actions that are realistic and supportable.
- Reviewer sign-off for material judgement.

## Blocking failures

- DTA recognised solely because management expects growth, without support.
- Draft or stale source-register rate used in a final tax provision.
- Current and deferred tax netted without a legal basis and same authority assessment.
