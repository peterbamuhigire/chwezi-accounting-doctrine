# Provision Recognition Decision Tree

Use this decision tree for provisions, contingent liabilities, and contingent assets.

## Liability side

| Step | Question | If yes | If no |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Is there a present legal or constructive obligation from a past event? | Go to step 2. | Do not recognise a provision. Consider contingent disclosure only if a possible obligation exists. |
| 2 | Is outflow probable? | Go to step 3. | Disclose contingent liability unless outflow is remote. |
| 3 | Can the amount be estimated reliably? | Recognise a provision. | Disclose contingent liability unless remote. |
| 4 | Is the time value of money material? | Discount using a supportable rate. | Measure at undiscounted best estimate. |

## Asset side

| Step | Question | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Is inflow virtually certain? | Recognise asset. |
| 2 | Is inflow probable but not virtually certain? | Disclose contingent asset. |
| 3 | Is inflow not probable? | No recognition and normally no disclosure. |

## Measurement rule

- Large population: expected-value method.
- Single obligation: most-likely outcome adjusted for risk where needed.
- Reimbursements: recognise only when virtually certain and present separately.

## Blockers

- Future operating losses cannot be provided for.
- General reserves or smoothing provisions are not provisions.
- Contingent liabilities cannot be recognised as provisions unless recognition criteria are met.
