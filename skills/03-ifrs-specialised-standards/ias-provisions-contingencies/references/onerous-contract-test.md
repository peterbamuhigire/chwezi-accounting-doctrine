# Onerous Contract Test

An onerous contract exists when unavoidable costs of meeting obligations exceed expected economic benefits.

## Required inputs

| Input | Rule |
|---|---|
| Contract identifier | Signed contract or enforceable arrangement. |
| Remaining obligations | Goods, services, time period, or minimum purchase commitment. |
| Expected benefits | Contract revenue or other economic benefits still expected. |
| Fulfilment costs | Direct costs and allocated costs that relate directly to the contract. |
| Exit costs | Penalties, termination payments, or unavoidable settlement costs. |
| Existing asset impairment | Test related assets before recognising the onerous-contract provision. |

## Calculation

| Line | Amount |
|---|---:|
| Cost to fulfil remaining obligation | 0 |
| Cost to exit contract | 0 |
| Unavoidable cost: lower of fulfil and exit | 0 |
| Expected economic benefits | 0 |
| Provision required | 0 |

## Journal pattern

| Account | Debit | Credit |
|---|---:|---:|
| Onerous contract expense | Provision required | 0 |
| Provision - onerous contract | 0 | Provision required |

## Controls

- A bad-margin contract is not onerous if benefits exceed unavoidable costs.
- Do not include avoidable overheads or future business losses outside the contract.
- Reassess the provision each reporting period and reverse unused amounts when obligation ends.
