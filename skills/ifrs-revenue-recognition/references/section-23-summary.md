# Section 23 Revenue Summary

IFRS for SMEs Section 23 recognises revenue when economic benefits are probable and the amount can be measured reliably. The detailed trigger depends on the transaction type.

## Sale of goods

Recognise revenue when all conditions are met:

- Significant risks and rewards of ownership have transferred to the buyer.
- The seller retains neither continuing managerial involvement nor effective control.
- Revenue can be measured reliably.
- Economic benefits are probable.
- Costs incurred or to be incurred can be measured reliably.

## Services

Use stage of completion when the outcome can be estimated reliably.

| Requirement | Evidence |
|---|---|
| Revenue measurable | Contract price or approved rate card. |
| Benefits probable | Customer credit and enforceable right to consideration. |
| Stage measurable | Milestone, time, output, or cost-to-cost measure. |
| Costs measurable | Project cost records and forecast to complete. |

If the outcome cannot be estimated reliably, recognise revenue only to the extent of recoverable expenses incurred.

## Construction-type contracts

Use percentage of completion when outcome is reliable. Expected losses are recognised immediately.

## Interest, royalties, dividends

| Type | Recognition |
|---|---|
| Interest | Effective-interest method. |
| Royalties | Accrual basis under agreement substance. |
| Dividends | When right to receive payment is established. |

## Controls

- Cash receipt alone is not a revenue trigger.
- VAT point of supply may differ from revenue recognition date.
- Deferred revenue is required where cash is received before performance.
