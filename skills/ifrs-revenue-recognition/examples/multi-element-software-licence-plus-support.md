# Example: Multi-Element Software Licence Plus Support

## Scenario

- Entity: full-IFRS software vendor.
- Contract date: 2026-04-01.
- Customer pays: 120,000,000 upfront.
- Promises:
  - Perpetual software licence delivered on 2026-04-01.
  - 12 months of support from 2026-04-01 to 2027-03-31.
- Stand-alone selling prices:
  - Licence: 100,000,000.
  - Support: 50,000,000.

## Allocation

| Performance obligation | SSP | Allocation percentage | Allocated price |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Licence | 100,000,000 | 66.67% | 80,000,000 |
| Support | 50,000,000 | 33.33% | 40,000,000 |
| Total | 150,000,000 | 100.00% | 120,000,000 |

## Recognition

| Performance obligation | Pattern | Revenue recognised at inception |
|---|---|---:|
| Licence | Point in time when control transfers | 80,000,000 |
| Support | Over time over 12 months | 0 at inception; 3,333,333 per month |

## Initial journal

| Account | Debit | Credit |
|---|---:|---:|
| Bank | 120,000,000 | 0 |
| Software licence revenue | 0 | 80,000,000 |
| Contract liability - support | 0 | 40,000,000 |

## Monthly support release

| Account | Debit | Credit |
|---|---:|---:|
| Contract liability - support | 3,333,333 | 0 |
| Support revenue | 0 | 3,333,333 |

## Pass criteria

- Performance obligations are distinct and documented.
- Allocation uses relative stand-alone selling prices.
- Support revenue is not recognised upfront.
- VAT or sales-tax timing is handled separately through the source-register-gated tax workflow.
