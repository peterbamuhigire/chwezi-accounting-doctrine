# Impairment Indicator Checklist

Run this checklist at each reporting date and whenever material adverse events occur.

## External indicators

| Indicator | Evidence |
|---|---|
| Market value declined materially beyond normal use | Valuation, market listing, auction evidence, or broker quote. |
| Technology, market, legal, or economic environment worsened | Board paper, market report, law change, customer loss, or regulator notice. |
| Interest rates increased enough to affect recoverable amount | Discount-rate update or lender rate evidence. |
| Listed entity market capitalisation is below carrying amount | Market data and net asset comparison. |

## Internal indicators

| Indicator | Evidence |
|---|---|
| Physical damage or obsolescence | Inspection report, maintenance log, or insurance claim. |
| Asset is idle, discontinued, or planned for disposal | Management plan, closure memo, or board approval. |
| Worse-than-expected operating performance | Actual versus budget, cash-flow shortfall, or margin decline. |
| Major restructuring affects asset use | Approved restructuring plan. |

## Decision result

| Result | Action |
|---|---|
| No indicator | Retain checklist as close evidence. |
| Indicator present | Prepare recoverable-amount workpaper. |
| Indicator confirmed and carrying amount exceeds recoverable amount | Post impairment loss. |
| Prior impairment indicator reversed | Test reversal, except goodwill impairment is never reversed. |

## Controls

- Goodwill under full IFRS still requires annual testing even without indicators.
- IFRS for SMEs goodwill follows indicator-based impairment with amortisation.
- Discount rates, fair values, and cash-flow forecasts require reviewer sign-off when material.
