# Uganda Public-Sector PFM Framework

Cross-cutting reference for Uganda central-government, local-government, and public-entity finance work. Use it whenever an engagement touches a Ugandan public body, a donor-funded government project, or a public-sector accounting/reporting/budgeting/procurement question.

**This reference captures the legal/framework *structure* and *citations*. It does not fix statutory rates or amounts that change by Act, regulation, or circular.** Rates, fees, and any amended deadline flow through the source register and `live-rate-verification-protocol.md`. Treat every figure below as "framework-as-issued, verify the current instrument before final output." See also `uganda-compliance-caveats.md` (tax/EFRIS) and `uganda-ngo-financial-management-patterns.md` (NGO sector).

## When this applies

| Body type | Basis | Primary instrument |
|---|---|---|
| Ministries, Agencies, LG votes (MALGs) | Modified accrual | MOFPED Financial Reporting Guide 2024; PFMA 2015 |
| Local governments (districts, municipalities, sub-counties, town/division councils, admin units) | Modified accrual / modified cash | Local Governments (Financial and Accounting) Regulations 2007 (SI 25/2007) under the Local Governments Act 1997 |
| Public corporations, state enterprises, government-controlled companies (>50% voting/board) | Full accrual (must state IFRS or IPSAS) | PFMA 2015 s.51; Companies Act |
| Donor-funded govt projects (GoU / GoU+DP / fully external) | Consolidated into supervising vote | MOFPED FRG 2024; donor agreement |

Government is on a **phased transition to accrual IPSAS**; default to the basis the controlling instrument prescribes for the entity type and disclose it.

## Legal framework hierarchy

1. **Constitution of Uganda** — art. 154(3) Grant of Credit; art. 164 pecuniary responsibility of accounting officers.
2. **Public Finance Management Act 2015 (as amended)** — the apex PFM statute. Key sections: s.15 (annual cash-flow plan), s.22 (virement), s.26 (Contingencies Fund), s.31–32 (withdrawal/warrant), s.34 (asset registers, board of survey), s.46(2) (authority to issue reporting guidelines), s.50 (reporting timelines), s.51 (Public Corp/SE reporting), s.52 (Accountant General consolidated FS), s.56–58 (Petroleum Fund).
3. **PFM Regulations 2016 (PFMR)** — e.g. reg. 37(3) board-of-survey report.
4. **Treasury Instructions 2017 (TIs)** — operational detail (e.g. par 16.29.15 investment register; sec 17.20 reconciliations; par 21 losses).
5. **MOFPED Financial Reporting Guide 2024 (rev. June 2024)** — issued by the Accountant General under PFMA s.46(2); prescribes statements, templates (FRTs), Chart of Accounts, and the annual Financial Reporting Circular.
6. **Local Governments Act 1997** + **Local Governments (Financial and Accounting) Regulations 2007 (SI 25/2007)** — LG financial control.
7. **PPDA Act 2003 (as amended, incl. the PPDA (Amendment) Act 2021)** with the **PPDA Regulations 2023** (gazetted 8 December 2023, effective 5 February 2024), the retained **PPDA Regulations 2014**, and the **PPDA Guidelines 2024** — procurement and disposal (thresholds and methods live here, *not* in SI 25/2007). The 2023 Regulations **harmonise central and local government** procurement under one framework and **revoked the former separate Local Governments (PPDA) Regulations 2006** (do not cite the 2006 regulations as operative). Administrative review now has two tiers (Accounting Officer, then the PPDA Appeals Tribunal). Verify the current consolidated text and any later amendments/guidelines before final output.
8. Entity-level **Accounting Manual** and **Internal Audit Manual** — lawful subsidiary documents (LG Regs reg. 61).

## Reporting calendar (central government, FY 1 Jul – 30 Jun)

| Report | Statutory deadline | Administrative (working) deadline |
|---|---|---|
| Semi-annual FS (period to 31 Dec) | 15 Feb | 30 Jan |
| 9-month FS (period to 31 Mar) | 15 May | 30 Apr |
| Annual FS (period to 30 Jun) | 31 Aug | 31 Jul |
| Accountant General → Auditor General (consolidated) | within 3 months of FY end (before 30 Sep) | — |

Default to the administrative deadline as the working target. Monthly bank reconciliation prepared within 10 days of month-end, frozen/validated by Head of Accounts by the 15th; all FY bank statements frozen by 31 July. Local governments under SI 25/2007 file final accounts to the Auditor General **within 3 months** of FY end (regs 5(2)(h), 73); LG FY follows the LG Act.

## Mandated financial statements (MALG level, MOFPED FRG 2024 §9.6)

Primary: (1) Statement of Financial Performance; (2) Statement of Financial Position; (3) Statement of Changes in Equity; (4) Cash Flow Statement (**direct method**, GoU-mandated); (5) Appropriation Account by NDP Programme; (6) Appropriation Account by Nature of Expenditure; (7) Statement of Reconciliation (Appropriation vs Financial Performance); (8) Statement of NTR collected; (9) Notes (policies + supporting); (10) Trial Balance + Detailed Trial Balance.

Memorandum: arrears of revenue; contingent liabilities (quantifiable and non-quantifiable); outstanding commitments; losses written off; losses reported not yet approved; stores/assets acquired; assets disposed. Consolidated-only: outstanding public debt; loans issued by government; investments held by government.

**Chart of Accounts:** GoU CoA is **43 digits / 9 segments** (adds Programmes and Geographical Location), issued and revised by the Accountant General. In LG, the CoA and accounting policy are issued by the Minister in consultation with the Accountant General (LG Regs reg. 60); no change to system/policy/basis without that approval.

## Budget execution and commitment control

- **Grant of Credit** (Auditor General, art. 154(3)) → **Minister's Warrant** (PFMA s.32, ≤ Grant of Credit) → **Accounting Warrant** to AO based on Expenditure Limit.
- Quarterly expenditure/cash limits issued by the 10th day of the first month of each quarter (PFMA s.15).
- **Virement** ≤ **10%** of an activity's allocation, with Minister approval (PFMA s.22). In LG (reg. 23–26): virement = reallocation within a sub-programme (excluding moves between employee costs and other charges), approved by the Chief Executive; re-allocation across programmes or between employee costs and other charges needs prior Executive Committee approval; supplementary estimates (increasing total approved expenditure) need prior Council approval.
- **Vote on account** ≤ **25%** of prior-year total revenue, valid **3 months** (LG reg. 21) — used when estimates are not yet approved.
- **Commitment Control System (CCS):** no LPO/contract/commitment without an approved commitment requisition and sufficient uncommitted balance in the quarter's limit; Head of Finance is personally liable for over-commitment (LG reg. 8).
- **Contingencies Fund** replenished to **0.5%** of prior-FY appropriated budget (PFMA s.26).
- Appropriations expire 30 June; unexpended balances revert to the Consolidated/UCF; DP balances are retained by the AO and must be re-voted before use.

## Local-government financial control (SI 25/2007) — distinctive rules

- **Banking intact:** all revenue banked intact; **no cash payments out of revenue collected**; only Central Bank legal tender; triplicate official receipts.
- **Signatories:** no councillor / chairperson / speaker may be a bank signatory or sign any council financial document; council accounts operated by Chief Executive + Head of Finance (project/agency accounts add a vote controller as third signatory).
- **Imprest:** special imprest retired by the date on the imprest warrant; general imprest retired by the last day of the FY; administrative advances accounted within 1 month; no new advance while a prior one is unretired.
- **Records retention:** vouchers/used receipt books — 6 years (after audit); principal accounting books — 15 years; personnel records — ≥30 years.
- **Board of survey:** appointed at FY end; cash survey on the first working day of the new FY (within 2 weeks); plus surprise surveys. (Central govt: board-of-survey report to Accountant General within 2 months of FY close.)
- **Surcharge / pecuniary liability (reg. 117–124):** officers are personally and pecuniarily responsible; Executive Committee recommends surcharge, LG PAC determines it, appeal to the Minister within 1 month, surcharge is a debt due within 1 month. Offences carry fines (≤250 currency points) and/or imprisonment (≤3 months); losses are a debt recoverable even from retired officers.
- **Asset register** mandatory (vehicles/plant, machinery/furniture, land & buildings with title deeds held by Head of Finance, inspected annually by the Auditor General).
- Internal audit is statutory, reports **directly to council** quarterly (within 1 month of quarter end), and audits procurement for value for money; the LG PAC examines Auditor General and internal-audit reports.

## Governance roles

- **Accounting Officer** (central: vote AO; LG: Chief Executive / CAO / Town Clerk) — overall control of financial transactions, asset management and registers, warrants, final accounts, vote controllers, salary currency; signs certificates (arrears, etc.).
- **Head of Finance / CFO** — receiver, paymaster, chief accountant; revenue collection and banking intact; commitment monitoring (personally liable for over-commitment); monthly/quarterly position to the Executive Committee; may object to unlawful directions in writing to the Auditor General.
- **Accountant General / Treasury** — issues the Reporting Guide, FRTs, annual Circular, CoA, accounting policies; centralises TSA reconciliations; prepares and submits consolidated FS; maintains the investment register; appoints boards of survey.
- **Auditor General** — Grant of Credit; audits the Petroleum Fund, Contingencies Fund, and consolidated GoU FS; full access rights.
- **Internal Audit, Executive Committee, Standing Committees, LG PAC** — oversight, surcharge, write-off, review of returns and audit reports.

## How to use in deliverables

- Identify the entity type first; it fixes the basis, the statements, and the calendar.
- Cite the controlling instrument and its section/regulation for every requirement asserted.
- Restate statutory **rates/fees** only from a verified-current source-register entry — never from this document or from a client's own manual.
- For a donor-funded government project, layer donor-agreement requirements on top of GoU requirements and reconcile the two.

## Forbidden patterns

- Asserting a Uganda statutory rate, fee, currency-point value, or amended deadline as current without a verified source-register entry.
- Presenting a framework figure here as if it overrides the current Act/regulation/circular.
- Mixing the modified-accrual MALG basis with the full-accrual public-corporation basis in one entity's statements.
- Treating a donor template as a substitute for the GoU statement set (both are required).
